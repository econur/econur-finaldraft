Why It Works

The Science Behind It

Most soap bars prioritise cleansing power. This one was formulated around a different question: what does shaving-stressed or sensitive skin actually need?

Low Cleansing Intensity

A cleansing value within the sensitive-safe range means it removes dirt and oil without over-stripping — preserving the lipid layer that holds your barrier together.

High Conditioning Value

A conditioning score well above the baseline translates directly into less post-wash tightness, fewer flake-outs, and more comfortable skin throughout the day.

Creamy, Cushioning Lather

High creaminess isn't just a luxury — for shaving skin, a dense, slip-rich lather reduces blade friction and the micro-abrasions that trigger inflammation.

Mild Stability Profile

A moderate hardness and stable INS value means the bar is firm enough for longevity, soft enough for face-friendly use — without the harshness of over-hardened soaps.

How to Use

Your Daily Ritual

1
Wet Your Skin

Rinse your face or body with lukewarm water. Avoid hot water — it opens pores aggressively and strips the natural oil barrier.

2
Build a Lather

Work the bar between your palms to build a rich, creamy lather. For shaving, apply the foam directly to the area before the blade — it cushions and protects.

3
Massage & Let It Sit

Gently massage in circular motions for 30–60 seconds. Let it rest briefly — the conditioning actives work best with a little contact time.

4
Rinse Thoroughly

Rinse with cool water to close pores and seal in softness. Pat dry gently — rubbing adds friction on already-sensitised skin.

5
Store It Dry

Rest on a draining soap dish between uses. A dry bar lasts significantly longer and stays hygienic.
